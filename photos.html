Framing

Framing a picture can get expensive. After reaching out to local photo shops and receiving outrageous estimates just to frame a picture, I decided to make my own. After committing to the plan, I went to the hardware store to get some supplies, including a saw, measuring tape, black paint, glue, wood and nails. When I got home and laid everything on the floor, I quickly realized I had no idea what I was doing.

I wanted to add a 2-inch thick backing to the image, but my lack of experience made it quite challenging. Once I started measuring and cutting pieces of wood, everything started falling into place. I cut out an outline of the picture with 2-inch thick wood and nailed it to a similar sized piece of plywood.

After seeing that the adhesive for the picture worked better with paint than without, I painted everything so the photo would mend well.

One of the most difficult parts of constructing this frame was gluing the picture to the plywood. The glue was extremely strong, giving you only one chance to place the image correctly. After getting it in place, I smoothed the image out and applied pressure to it. One advice I would give is to never throw out your expensive college text books, because you never know when you'll need them again.

Once the glue dried, I trimmed the excess edges and strung wire in the back so the picture could be hung. Though it was a lot of work, building my own frame was far less expensive than paying someone to frame it for me. Plus, it was much more rewarding in the end.
